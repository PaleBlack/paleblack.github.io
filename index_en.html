Biography

It all began with two friends from the Emmental who shared a passion for metal

In 2009 the two friends, Thomas and Sebastian decided to start a metal band. They practiced hard, and searched for additional band members. With the most minimalistic advert ever "death metal band seeks drummer", they caught Dani's attention in the summer of 2011. It was soon evident that they shared not only the same sense of humor, but also the same musical vision. The three threw out all the old songs, and got busy creating new ones. Once they had the basics, they started looking for a bass guitarist and a singer. During this time they met many musicians, but only found people to fill the gap in the winter of 2013. When they met Miriam, her solo performance of Janice Joplin's " Mercedes Benz" convinced them immediately. She soon learnt how to growl, and together with Bruno, the band was finally complete. They practiced hard in the Emmental, and half a year later, Pale Black was on stage for the first time. Since then they have enjoyed doing more gigs and have also written more songs. An EP as well as a music video will follow soon. So stay in touch.

Members

These five cheerful souls rarely ever take anything serious, except for their music

Vocals: Miriam

The only woman in the band can hold her own against the guys thanks to her powerful voice. She likes abstract jokes!z.

Guitar: Thomas

He, together with Sebastian, is responsible for the guitar wall that Pale Black blasts at you. His huge passion for metal is the driving force behind the band.

Bass: Bruno

As soon as he gets hold of a bass guitar, the quiet, calm Bruno changes into an animal. His tempo stability helps Dani stay in time and he thrills the audiences with his melodies on the frettless bass.

Guitar: Sebastian

Although he will drum out a rhythm during every break, he is a true guitarist. He usually provides the basis for the songs together with Thomas.

Drums: Dani

He is lovingly called the band granddad by the rest of the band. He causes some confusion with his visions of the order of beats and polyrhythms.
